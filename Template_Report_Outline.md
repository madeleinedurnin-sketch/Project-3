## 1. Introduction (approx. 1 page)
*   **Framing:** Introduce the specific social issue (e.g., gender representation, esports legitimacy).
*   **Relevance:** Why does this matter in the current sport landscape?
*   **Research Question:** State clearly what you are investigating.

## 2. Related Literature (approx. 2 pages)
*   ***New Section:*** Connect your project to 2-3 key concepts or readings from the course.
*   **Theory:** What sociological framework are you using? (e.g., Hegemonic Masculinity, Institutional Theory).
*   **Gap:** What do we already know, and what is your data adding?

## 3. Data & Materials (approx. 1 page)
*   **Data Source:** Describe the dataset (Platform, dates, volume of data).
*   **Scope:** What specific subset did you focus on? (e.g., "Top 100 comments," "Posts from Oct 2023").
*   **Cleaning:** Briefly mention how you prepared the data (removed bots, filtered languages).

## 4. Analytical Approach & Justification (approx. 2 pages)
*   **Method:** What operationalization choices did you make? (How did you define "positive sentiment" or "skill-based language"?).
*   **Justification:** *Why* is this method appropriate? (e.g., "We used keyword tagging to identify patterns of...")
*   **Process:** Describe the steps taken to analyze the data.

## 5. Results (approx. 1 page)
*   **Presentation:** Present your findings clearly. Use tables or simple charts if helpful.
*   **Description:** Describe the patterns found in the data. Do NOT interpret them yet—just state the facts.

## 6. Interpretation & Discussion (approx. 2 pages)
*   **Meaning:** What do these results *mean* in the context of sport sociology?
*   **Connection:** Link back to your Introduction and Literature.
*   **Implications:** What does this suggest for sport managers, athletes, or society?

## 7. Ethical Reflection & Limitations (approx. 1 page)
*   **Limitations:** What could you *not* see with this data? (e.g., sarcasm detection, missing demographics).
*   **Ethics:** Discuss one specific ethical challenge you faced (bias, privacy) and how you navigated it.
